What you need to know to stay protected

Major browsers such as Google Chrome have been restricting adblockers. Many mobile devices now have no adbocker support.

This had led to a rise in malware on mobile devices. Increased reliance on smartphones has also increased the market for mobile malware. You risk having your information stolen, corrupted, or held for ransom.

This includes information such as:

- Online banking accounts

- Social media profiles

- Personal files such as pictures or important downloads

- Don't let malicious actors catch you off guard

What are the odds?

In Fiscal 2024 Q1: The most common threat to mobile devices is adware. Making up over 46% of all malware

Over 389,000 malware installation programs were detected

Almost 12,000 of these packages targeted mobile banking information

2,000 of these installation packages were Trojan Ransomware
